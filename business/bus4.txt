A tripartite collaboration was signed to promote hybrid crop seed of Corteva Agrisicence in Bangladesh yesterday.
Petrochem Bangladesh Limited, Corteva Agriscience, and USAID’s Feed the Future Bangladesh Rice and Diversified Crop (RDC) signed the agreement at a hotel in the capital yesterday, said a press release.
Through the collaboration, USAID and Corteva and Petrochem will ensure high quality seed for farmers of Bangladesh for quality crop production, added the statement.
Charles J Hall, president and CEO of ACDI/VOCA, Cuan Opperman, chief of party of USAID’s Feed the Future Bangladesh Rice and Diversified Crop (RDC) Activity, attended the programme.
