Venezuela's leader has lauded food exports on television as a way to raise hard currency to stabilize an economy in crisis.
Venezuela remains almost entirely dependent on oil exports, which amounted to $29 billion last year.
Maduro and his predecessor, Hugo Chavez, for years accused food companies of hoarding and profiteering.
