Spot gold hit its highest since Oct. 10 at $1,504.35, but was down slightly by 0312 GMT, at $1,501.70 per ounce.
This is the second time in past two weeks that spot gold has crossed the $1,500 level.
Holdings of the largest gold-backed exchange-traded-fund (ETF), New York's SPDR Gold Trust, fell 0.13 per cent on Wednesday from Tuesday.
Elsewhere, palladium hit an all-time high of $1,785.50 on supply concerns and was last up 0.2 per cent at $1,780.55 an ounce.
Platinum was up about 0.1 per cent at $924.40, after scaling a more than three-week high in the previous session.
