Rajandra Shrestha, a Gree sales agent in Nepal, shared the secret of Gree's rising in a recent interview with Xinhua.
In 2018, two-way trade between China and Nepal reached 1.1 billion U.S. dollars, marking an 11.7-percent increase year on year.
"I see more and more 'Made in China' products in Nepali people's lives," said Yubaraj Sangroula, a Kathmandu-based professor.
"China has advantages in science and technology," Ashok Kumar Shrestha, senior vice president of Nepal Trans Himalaya Border Commerce Association, told Xinhua.
"For Nepal, we need Chinese technology, which can facilitate us to realize our vision of 'Prosperous Nepal, Happy Nepalis.'
