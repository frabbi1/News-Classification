The indigenous communities of the tea gardens in Sylhet celebrated their largest traditional festival, Karam Utshab, with a call for the enactment of a law to protect their tradition, culture, language and environment.
On the opening night, the community people, led by the chief, prayed under the ‘Holy Karam’ tree.
At sunset, an altar was built, where they planted the branches of the Karam tree.
The event ended on Tuesday morning, through the immersion of branches of the Karam tree in the local canal.
