Maritime ports of Chattogram, Cox’s Bazar, Mongla and Payra have been asked to keep hoisted local cautionary signal number three as squally weather is likely to affect the areas.
The cyclonic storm “Bulbul” over the east-central bay and adjoining areas moved slightly north-northwestwards over the same area at 12 pm today.
The Met Office said in a special weather bulletin that it was centred about 865 km south-southwest of Chattogram port, 795 km south-southwest of cox’s bazar port, 830 km south of Mongla port and 790 km south of Payra port.
The maximum sustained wind speed within 54 kmh of the cyclone centre is about 62 kph rising to 88 kph in gusts or squalls.
The sea will remain very rough near the cyclone centre, the Met Office added.
