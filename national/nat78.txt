As the cyclonic storm “Bulbul” is likely to hit Bangladesh, the government has already taken all-out preparations to deal with the rushing cyclonic storm.
“Necessary steps have been taken from our side so that people and their livestock near coastal areas can safely take refuse in the cyclone shelters as there is remaining danger signal number seven in the coastal areas of the country,” he said.
Bangladesh Water Development Board (BWDB) has taken pre-cautionary measures and hence directed all its employees in coastal areas to remain at offices till November 16 to combat cyclonic storm ‘Bulbul’.
‘Warning Campaigns’ have been strengthened in the coastal districts and upazilas for the cyclonic storm ‘Bulbul’, he said adding that Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina has directed the people of the disaster-prone districts to preserve foods and necessary products in the ‘Paribarik Sailo’.
Chairman of Parliamentary Standing Committee on Disaster Management and Relief Ministry Captain (Retd) AB Tajul Islam, Senior Secretary of the Ministry of Disaster Management and Relief Md Shah Kamal and other senior officials were present on the occasion.
